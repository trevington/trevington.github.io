01 // PACKAGING & STRUCTURAL

Mechanical Design

Physical packaging structures, self-jigging enclosures, structural chassis interfaces, and cell basket manufacturing tolerances.

Self-Jigging Enclosure Assembly

To eliminate the complex external clamping setups that caused severe panel warping during the CR29 build, the CR30 welded aluminum enclosure utilizes waterjet-cut slot and tab features integrated directly along the panel seams. Internal walls feature precision slots that receive matching tabs on external panels, locking the raw structural sheets square for immediate tack-welding without external fixtures.

Manufacturing Retrospective

Corner pieces were omitted from the self-jigging pattern. Post-weld distortion pulled one corner visibly inward, compressing the available volume for the high-voltage distribution layout and requiring an emergency tight-tolerance redesign of the Power Prism sleeve component.

PAHT-CF High-Temperature Cell Enclosures

Each of the 5 module baskets is a structural 3D-printed shell holding 28 pouch cells in a rigid matrix. Transitioning away from brittle polycarbonate, PAHT-CF (High-Temperature Carbon Fiber Nylon) was selected for its superior impact resistance during localized destruction testing. Structural retention features include an open-face close-out plate that sandwiches the cell group, and dedicated lifting provisions designed to drop directly into the integrated vertical retention stack holes for quick-extraction modular servicing.

Structural Monocoque Interfaces

The entire accumulator system mechanical load transfers to the vehicle monocoque through 10 bolted points (4 rear, 6 front). Fasteners anchor directly into pre-preg composite inserts (pucks) positioned inside the carbon laminate prior to autoclaving. To secure physical structural equivalency certification (SES) after manufacturing deviations, mechanical shims and ground aluminum block spacers were integrated to bridge a step height discrepancy caused by proud front puck surfaces.

03 // PROPULSION & DISTRO

Motor & Inverter

Tractive drive units, corner high-voltage distribution components, and specialized ampacity busbar conductor calculations.

Corner HV Distribution Sleeve

Departing from bulky, long Coroflex high-voltage cable runs, CR30 consolidates primary tractive components into a tight structural 3D-printed sleeve isolated in the corner of the pack. This single localized unit packages the main air contactors, the principal system fuse, and the hardline interconnect distribution busbars.

Transient Thermal Evaluation ($\frac{\rho \cdot L}{A}$)

Standard static ampacity tables are highly conservative for competition cars that only draw maximum current during brief acceleration states. Conductor geometries were downsized significantly by evaluating heat generation using transient thermodynamic resistance equations matched directly against endurance-duration ambient convection values.

BMB Stiffness & Pogo Pin Rescue Protocol

The raw, unsupported center span of the 5 internal Battery Management Boards warped downward under the internal spring loading of the cell sense pogo pins, severing electrical continuity. The solution deployed was a mechanical retention stack retrofit assembly—combining a steel rod, a custom plastic insulator profile, and a horizontal stiffener component to pull the board structural plane back into a flush, flat orientation.
